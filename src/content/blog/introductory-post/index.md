For those of you who missed it, I'm currently a college student studying Computer Science. I started programming seriously in high school, starting out with Processing, a visual programming language before transitioning to Java. My early days were spent solving leetcode-style questions (mostly in the form of string manipulation and dynamic lists) and building CLIs (nothing too crazy, just prompting and messing around with user input).

Heading into my senior year, I started to explore mobile (and web, but this was more of a sidequest) development and built my first Android app intended to clone WhatsApp with (you guessed it) Firebase and Flutter. This is around the same time I worked on a parking system for my high school and some stuff to experiment with Websockets, Express, and Node (default Javascript for server apps). These experiences were really rewarding and I decided to jump into the rabbit hole of software architecture and design patterns (mostly on the small-scale side of this though).

So far, I'm still at the tip of the iceberg and I've been having a blast working with open source tools from HackerNews and personal Googling. My goal is to have a really good idea of use cases for stuff like databases, ORMs, and middleware and how to ensure that these technologies are built to scale. Concepts in low-level computing and higher-order mathematics are also of interest to me, although I see them as more of a side quest.

In my spare time I like to read blogs and papers, workout, and occasionally practice violin. I haven't decided on a posting schedule for my blog so content distribution will be sporadic. I'm also open to suggestions on what to write about so feel free to reach out to me on my socials. Hopefully I can find some interesting topics to write about, but if not, I might as well talk about what I'm working on projects-wise and flesh out the nitty-gritty details.
